Exhibit L · Enforcement Precedent

1704 Westover Rd, Bryker Woods — Ordered Demolished After a Four-Year Code Battle

Andrea Ball, “Four years. Dozens of inspections. One dilapidated house finally marked for demolition,” Austin Current, July 6, 2026.
Original: austincurrent.org/2026/07/06/austin-demolition-abandoned-citycode-compliance-bryker
Archived reference. This page is a faithful, attributed summary of the news report cited above, preserved so the precedent survives link changes. It is not the full article; please read the original at the link above. Quotations are reproduced as reported by Austin Current.

The City of Austin's Building and Standards Commission (BSC) is the enforcement body the 7609 Creekbluff report asks the City to use. The Bryker Woods case shows that path working on a closely comparable property: a long-vacant, deteriorated single-family home that neighbors had complained about for years.

The property

  • Address: 1704 Westover Rd., Bryker Woods, Austin
  • Owner: Polly Nance (moved out in 2008 to care for her mother in Pecos)
  • Built 1939 · 1,890 sq ft · ~$1.2 million (Travis Central Appraisal District)

Enforcement timeline

  • Feb 8, 2022 — first formal complaint ("this is an ongoing problem"); initial inspection Feb 9 by Mitchell Alvarez.
  • March 2022 — citation issued for broken fence and siding violations.
  • March 2022 – February 2025 — the City conducted 33 follow-up inspections.
  • 2024 — case taken to municipal court; the owner failed to appear; a judge issued an arrest warrant.
  • Nov 21, 2025 — Inspector Erica Thompson obtained a search warrant; documented a broken front door and an interior full of boxes, chemicals, trash, and exposed insulation.
  • January 2026 — the City cleared the property as a health hazard (animal droppings, mold, rotting food).
  • May 2026 — the Building and Standards Commission (case 2026‑013540) voted unanimously to demolish the structure and authorized the City to bill the owner; demolition expected roughly October 2026 or later.

Documented conditions

Rats, raccoons, and possums flourishing and crossing to neighboring properties; peeling paint and rotted wood; trees grown into the porch and a fallen tree in a neighbor's yard; a removed electrical meter; and an interior of accumulated trash, chemicals, mold, and rotting food.

Neighbors & the community

The lead complainant, Cori Modisett, lives across the street and had first observed rats years earlier. An 11‑year‑old neighbor testified to the Commission about her fear that wildlife on the property would attack her small dog. After the vote, Modisett said the neighbors would keep watch: “We will stay on top of them.”

Key quotes (as reported)

The City has “three enforcement avenues” for non-compliant homeowners: the Building and Standards Commission, an administrative hearing, or municipal court.— City spokeswoman Stephanie Sánchez
The owner “was appalled to know the condition in which this property is currently in.”— Inspector Erica Thompson (case log)
“They had my email address, my phone number. I mean, I'm not hiding from anybody. I'm simply not.”— Owner Polly Nance, disputing the City's account that she was unresponsive

Why it matters for 7609 Creekbluff

Bryker Woods establishes that Austin will pursue a long-vacant, deteriorated home all the way to a Building and Standards Commission demolition order — the exact remedy the Creekbluff report requests. The same inspector, Erica Thompson, documented both properties. One contrast cuts in the neighborhood's favor: the Westover owner is an elderly, disabled individual who disputes being unresponsive, whereas Creekbluff's owners of record are a developer and a related-party LLC — a less sympathetic posture for resisting the same enforcement.
